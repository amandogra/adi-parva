Chapter 2

The Bharata Lineage

◀ Prev Next ▶

Before the princes of the great war were born, the line of the Bharatas stretched back through kings who wrestled with promises, rituals, and the fragile work of holding a realm together.

Among them shone King Shantanu of the Kuru line, whose river-bound marriage first, and later forest-bound love, would each send ripples down the generations.

From his union with the river goddess came a son, Devavrata, trained in arms and scripture, whose strength and clarity of mind made him the natural heir to the throne.

Years later, Shantanu met a fisher-chief's daughter whose father demanded that only a son born to her should inherit, a condition that threatened Devavrata's succession and the king's happiness alike.

To protect his father's joy and the line of the Bharatas, Devavrata vowed to renounce both the throne and marriage, accepting a life of celibacy so that no child of his could ever claim the crown.
This terrible vow would earn him the name Bhishma, “he of the fearsome oath”.

The heavens trembled at the severity of the vow, and the throne was secured for the yet-unborn child of the fisher-maiden, while Bhishma took upon himself the duty of guarding the kingdom.

In time, two princes were born to Shantanu and his queen: Chitrangada and Vichitravirya, whose brief lives and untimely deaths would leave the royal line dangerously thin.

It is into this narrowing branch of the Bharata tree that the next generation must arrive, through choices and arrangements that test the limits of custom and sacrifice.
